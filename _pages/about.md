I am **Zihao Huang (黄子豪)**, a **Faculty member (Lecturer)** at **Zhejiang A&F University**, working on forest resource remote sensing, land use/cover change simulation, forest age dynamics, and forest carbon cycle modeling.

My research connects multi-source remote sensing, geospatial modeling, machine learning, and ecological process models to understand how land-cover change and forest age dynamics affect subtropical forest carbon cycles. I develop and apply workflows based on Google Earth Engine, Python, cellular automata / FLUS-style LUCC models, LandTrendr / CCDC disturbance detection, BIOME-BGC / InTEC process modeling, and RF, XGBoost, CNN, and LSTM methods.

I received my Ph.D. in Forestry from Zhejiang A&F University in 2025 and was a visiting scholar at the **University of Eastern Finland** from 2024 to 2025, focusing on deep learning and artificial intelligence applications in Finnish forestry.

My work has produced first-author publications in journals including **IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing**, **Agricultural and Forest Meteorology**, **Geo-spatial Information Science**, **Remote Sensing**, **Ecological Indicators**, and **ISPRS International Journal of Geo-Information**. I am interested in collaboration on forest remote sensing, LUCC simulation, forest carbon accounting, and reproducible geospatial Python tooling.
